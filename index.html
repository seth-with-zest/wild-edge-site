Who's hosting?

I'm Seth. AI creates the perfect intersection of several things I've long loved and practiced. I'm creating The Wild Edge to be the place where it all distills into service for people who feel disoriented or aimless because of the AI moment we're in.

What I love:

  1. Gadgetry and technology

    My parents have said my first sentence was, "You push the button." AI is the gadget that reinvents itself each week. It feels like grabbing a high-voltage wire.

  2. Collaborative, exploratory, inventive conversation

    We are our best selves when a person brings it out of us. Whether it's using post-its, paints, or breakout rooms, there's a benevolently manipulative art to helping people realize that they can trust the person sitting across from them. Workplaces don't make space for it, but I can't imagine anything more important, and it's the craft I'm most proud of.

  3. Making order of mess

    I've called it systems thinking, business operations, empathy, sense-making, metaphor, and more. I see the fuzzy whole and find the dynamic underneath. It could be a situation, an initiative, a team, or a person. Nothing has received more of my analysis than my own mind, but I'm insatiably inquisitive about anything that's calling to be disentangled.

The AI moment calls for these three things because:

  1. We need a place to understand AI itself. The more updates you read about AI, the more you learn, but somehow you end up feeling less oriented and with more FOMO, not the opposite.
  2. We need a place to design our own relationship with AI. The invention of a cognitive tool creates something that feels like a cognitive identity crisis: "Where do my skills fit, now? Do I fit anywhere? AI lets me do more than I could before, but now I'm not even sure what I want to want."

AI can multiply your humanity instead of stealing it, but you need to tell it your very human motivations and perspective: what to think about, why, and how.

AI gives us the chance to reimagine and rebuild who we get to be, and how life and work get to feel. hallelujah.

But that's impossibly difficult to reckon with alone.

Your sense of perspective is all knotted up inside of you, and you can't untangle a rope under tension. I've made The Wild Edge as a place where you can take a break from "doing human" so that you can "be human," just for a spell. It gives you the space to wonder hard enough and explore with enough adventure to put words to what you find, and determine how to bring those words to AI.

You get to do it with people who are doing the same, myself included. Life is hard to understand, even without AI, but thankfully everyone else needs just as much help to understand it as you do.

If we have to go through an identity crisis, let's do it together. People need each other, these days.
